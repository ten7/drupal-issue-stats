Methodology notes. Data comes from the public Drupal.org api-d7 REST API. “Time to close” uses each issue’s last-modified timestamp as a proxy for its close date, so late edits to old closed issues can inflate it. “Fixed” (status 2) is counted as resolved even though it auto-closes to “Closed (fixed)” later. Author and commenter usernames are resolved only for the top contributors to keep the crawl polite. Support posture (current vs. legacy/EOL) is a heuristic on the branch number and doesn't account for a project's own supported-branch declarations. The resolution-time trend groups issues by the year they were opened, so recent cohorts contain only the faster-resolved issues and can look artificially quick.
